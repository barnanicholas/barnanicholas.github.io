About Me

My name is Nicholas Heinrich-Barna, and I am a first-year PhD candidate. I graduated in the spring of 2023 from TAMU with a double major in Computer Engineering and Petroleum Engineering. When I first entered university, I knew only broadly that I wanted to do something with math. Engineering seemed like a good choice, a broad field with numerous possibilities. Like most, it was during the course of my undergraduate studies that I honed in on my passion.
By navigating two degrees, I began to see all disciplines as a collection of interconnected systems. From typical computer programming to drilling engineering, I had the advantage of learning a large breadth of materials. Over time, I began to see patterns arise in all of my engineering classes. These patterns let me see the world of engineering less as separate distinct disciplines and more as interconnected systems wherein the decisions made in one area could affect the outcomes in a completely separate area. By evaluating these relationships, I began to believe these connections were a potential site for malpractice and malfeasance. This realization was pivotal in leading me to one of the most vital and influential industries today: Cyber Security

Current Research

Modern embedded systems, vital across industries like healthcare, transportation, and communication, heavily depend on hardware as the root of trust. Commercial products from industry giants like Intel, AMD, TI, and ARM are central to these systems. However, the increasing complexity of hardware designs has given rise to security-critical vulnerabilities that disrupt conventional threat models. Cross-layer attacks, which exploit both hardware and software, pose significant threats. To address this challenge, I propose the development of a hybrid hardware verification framework called VeriFuzz. This framework aims to combine the strengths of formal verification and hardware fuzzing to efficiently detect and mitigate security vulnerabilities in complex embedded systems, such as modern processors.
